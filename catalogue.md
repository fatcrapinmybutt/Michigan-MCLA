## 1. Michigan State Court Abbreviations

Michigan's courts form the **"One Court of Justice"** system established by the Michigan Constitution of 1963, Article VI, § 1.

| Abbreviation | Full Name | Acronym | Tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mich.** | Michigan Supreme Court | MSC | Supreme Court |
| **Mich. App.** | Michigan Court of Appeals | COA | Appellate Court |
| **Cir. Ct.** | Circuit Court | — | Trial Court – General |
| **Dist. Ct.** | District Court | — | Trial Court – Limited |
| **Prob. Ct.** | Probate Court | — | Trial Court – Limited |
| **Mun. Ct.** | Municipal Court | — | Trial Court – Local |
| **JTC** | Judicial Tenure Commission | — | Administrative / Oversight Body |

---

### Mich. — Michigan Supreme Court (MSC)

The **Michigan Supreme Court** is the highest court in Michigan. It has **general superintending control over all courts** in the state, possesses final appellate jurisdiction, and its opinions are cited using the reporter abbreviation **Mich.**

> *Constitutional Basis (One Court of Justice):* Mich. Const. 1963, art. VI, § 1  
> *Constitutional Basis (Superintending Control):* Mich. Const. 1963, art. VI, § 4  
> *Note:* Art. VI, § 4 provides in part: "the supreme court shall have general superintending control over all courts; power to issue, hear and determine prerogative and remedial writs; and appellate jurisdiction as provided by rules of the supreme court."  
> *Source:* Michigan Legislature, <https://legislature.mi.gov/Laws/MCL?objectName=mcl-Article-VI-4>

---

### Mich. App. — Michigan Court of Appeals (COA)

The **Michigan Court of Appeals** is the intermediate appellate court. It hears appeals from circuit courts, probate courts, and certain administrative agencies. Opinions are cited as **Mich. App.**

> *Constitutional Basis:* Mich. Const. 1963, art. VI, § 1

---

### Cir. Ct. — Circuit Court

The **Circuit Court** is the highest trial court in Michigan, with general original jurisdiction over:
- Civil claims **exceeding $25,000** (*see* MCL 600.8301(1) for district court's exclusive jurisdiction up to $25,000, which demarcates the circuit court threshold)
- Felony criminal cases
- Family law matters (divorce, custody, parenting time)
- Appeals from district and probate courts
- Appeals from certain administrative agencies

> *Constitutional Basis:* Mich. Const. 1963, art. VI, § 11

---

### Dist. Ct. — District Court

Often called the **"people's court"**, the District Court is the most frequently used court in Michigan. It handles:
- **Civil claims up to $25,000** — MCL 600.8301(1): "The district court has exclusive jurisdiction in civil actions when the amount in controversy does not exceed $25,000.00." *(Source: Michigan Legislature, MCL 600.8301)*
- Misdemeanor criminal offenses
- Civil infractions (including traffic tickets)
- Landlord-tenant disputes
- Small claims matters (see Small Claims division below; separate jurisdictional limit under MCL 600.8401)

> *Constitutional Basis:* Mich. Const. 1963, art. VI, § 15  
> *Jurisdictional Authority:* MCL 600.8301(1), Revised Judicature Act of 1961, Act 236 of 1961  
> *Source:* <https://www.legislature.mi.gov/Laws/MCL?objectName=mcl-600-8301>

---

### Prob. Ct. — Probate Court

The **Probate Court** has exclusive jurisdiction over:
- Administration of decedents' estates
- Trusts
- Mental health proceedings (involuntary hospitalization)
- Guardianships and conservatorships
- Matters involving minors

> *Constitutional Basis:* Mich. Const. 1963, art. VI, § 15

---

### Mun. Ct. — Municipal Court

**Municipal Courts** are retained by a small number of cities. These courts predate the district court system established in 1968 and continue to operate under local authority, handling local ordinance violations and limited civil matters.

> *Note:* Most municipal courts were replaced by the district court system. The continued existence of specific municipal courts should be confirmed with SCAO or the applicable local court before filing. **[UNVERIFIED: current exhaustive list of retained municipal courts]**

---

### JTC — Judicial Tenure Commission

The **Judicial Tenure Commission** is an independent body that investigates complaints against judges in Michigan. The JTC has authority to recommend discipline—including removal from office—to the Michigan Supreme Court.

> *Constitutional Basis:* Mich. Const. 1963, art. VI, § 30

### U.S. — United States Supreme Court

The **United States Supreme Court** is the highest court in the federal judiciary and the final authority on federal constitutional questions. Its decisions are binding on all courts throughout the United States.

> *Constitutional Basis:* U.S. Const. art. III, § 1  
> *Citation Example:* *Marbury v. Madison*, 5 U.S. (1 Cranch) 137 (1803)

---

### 6th Cir. — U.S. Court of Appeals for the Sixth Circuit

The **Sixth Circuit** is the federal intermediate appellate court covering **Michigan, Ohio, Kentucky, and Tennessee**. Its decisions are binding on all federal district courts within those four states.

> *Location:* Cincinnati, Ohio  
> *Citation Example:* *Smith v. Jones*, 123 F.3d 456 (6th Cir. 2020)  
> *Source:* <https://www.ca6.uscourts.gov/>

---

### E.D. Mich. — U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Michigan

The federal trial court for the **eastern portion of Michigan**, with its principal courthouse in Detroit, Michigan.

> *Primary Courthouse:* Detroit, Michigan  
> *Citation Example:* *Doe v. Roe*, 456 F. Supp. 3d 789 (E.D. Mich. 2021)  
> *Note:* Specific division locations and covered counties should be confirmed at <https://www.mied.uscourts.gov/> before filing. **[UNVERIFIED: exhaustive list of division cities for E.D. Mich.]**

---

### W.D. Mich. — U.S. District Court for the Western District of Michigan

The federal trial court for the **western portion of Michigan**.

> *Primary Courthouse:* Grand Rapids, Michigan  
> *Division Locations:* Grand Rapids, Kalamazoo, Lansing, and Marquette  
> *Citation Example:* *Doe v. Roe*, 789 F. Supp. 3d 123 (W.D. Mich. 2021)  
> *Source:* <https://www.miwd.uscourts.gov/>

## 3. Court Rule & Administrative Authorities

The primary rule sets and administrative bodies governing the Michigan "One Court of Justice" system.

| Abbreviation | Full Name | Type | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MCR** | Michigan Court Rules | Rule Set | General procedure in all Michigan courts |
| **MRE** | Michigan Rules of Evidence | Rule Set | Evidentiary standards in all Michigan courts |
| **MRPC** | Michigan Rules of Professional Conduct | Rule Set | Professional ethics for Michigan-licensed attorneys |
| **MCJC** | Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct | Rule Set | Professional ethics for Michigan judges |
| **SCAO** | State Court Administrative Office | Administrative Body | Statewide court administration and standardized forms |

---

### MCR — Michigan Court Rules

The **Michigan Court Rules** are the principal set of procedural rules governing practice and procedure in all Michigan courts. Promulgated by the Michigan Supreme Court, the MCR covers pleadings, motions, discovery, trials, and appeals.

> *Promulgated by:* Michigan Supreme Court  
> *Citation Example:* MCR 2.116 (motion for summary disposition)

---

### MRE — Michigan Rules of Evidence

The **Michigan Rules of Evidence** govern the admissibility of evidence in Michigan courts. Modeled in part on the Federal Rules of Evidence, the MRE controls what evidence may be presented to the trier of fact at trial.

> *Promulgated by:* Michigan Supreme Court  
> *Citation Example:* MRE 803 (hearsay exceptions)

---

### MRPC — Michigan Rules of Professional Conduct

The **Michigan Rules of Professional Conduct** are the ethical rules governing attorneys licensed in Michigan. Violations may result in discipline by the Attorney Grievance Commission and the Attorney Discipline Board.

> *Promulgated by:* Michigan Supreme Court  
> *Enforcement:* Attorney Grievance Commission / Attorney Discipline Board  
> *Citation Example:* MRPC 1.7 (conflict of interest)

---

### MCJC — Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct

The **Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct** sets ethical standards governing the conduct of all judges in Michigan. It is enforced through the Judicial Tenure Commission (JTC).

> *Promulgated by:* Michigan Supreme Court  
> *Enforcement:* Judicial Tenure Commission (JTC)

---

### SCAO — State Court Administrative Office

The **State Court Administrative Office** is the administrative arm of the Michigan Supreme Court. It is responsible for:
- Providing administrative support to the Michigan court system
- Collecting and publishing court statistics
- Developing and maintaining standard court forms used statewide

> *Parent Body:* Michigan Supreme Court  
> *Web:* <https://www.courts.michigan.gov/scao/>

## 4. Administrative Jurisdictions: MDHHS / MOAHR / APA

Michigan administrative proceedings operate under a distinct authority framework separate from the court system. This section maps the key administrative jurisdictions relevant to MDHHS benefit litigation and other state agency hearing pathways.

---

### Distinction: Agencies vs. Courts vs. Rule Sets

| Category | Examples | Role in Litigation |
|---|---|---|
| **Agency** | MDHHS, LARA, MOAHR | Issues decisions, conducts hearings, and administers programs |
| **Court** | Circuit Court, Court of Appeals | Reviews final agency decisions via judicial review (MCL 24.301 et seq.) |
| **Rule Set** | MI Admin Code R 792.10101 et seq., APA of 1969 | Governs procedure for administrative hearings |

---

### APA — Michigan Administrative Procedures Act of 1969

The **Michigan Administrative Procedures Act of 1969** (PA 306, MCL 24.201 *et seq.*) is the foundational statute for all administrative agency proceedings in Michigan. It establishes baseline requirements for:
- Rulemaking (MCL 24.231 et seq.)
- **Contested cases** — defined in MCL 24.203(3) as "a proceeding, including, but not limited to, rate-making, price-fixing, and licensing, in which a determination of the legal rights, duties, or privileges of a named party is required by law to be made by an agency after an opportunity for an evidentiary hearing"
- Notice, evidence, and hearing procedures (MCL 24.271–24.288)
- Agency decisions and judicial review (MCL 24.301 et seq.)

> *Authority:* MCL 24.201 et seq. (PA 306 of 1969)  
> *Source:* <https://www.legislature.mi.gov/Laws/MCL?objectName=mcl-Act-306-of-1969>

---

### MOAHR — Michigan Office of Administrative Hearings and Rules

The **Michigan Office of Administrative Hearings and Rules (MOAHR)** is an agency within the **Michigan Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs (LARA)**. MOAHR centralizes administrative hearings for numerous state agencies, including MDHHS. It promulgates and administers the **Michigan Administrative Hearing System (MAHS) Uniform Hearing Rules** (MI Admin Code R 792.10101 *et seq.*).

> *Parent Department:* Michigan Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs (LARA)  
> *Web:* <https://www.michigan.gov/lara/bureau-list/moahr>  
> *Type:* Administrative Agency / Hearing Body (NOT a court)

---

### MAHS Uniform Hearing Rules — Part 1 (General Scope)

**MI Admin Code R 792.10101** establishes the **scope** of the Michigan Administrative Hearing System Uniform Hearing Rules: these rules govern all MOAHR administrative hearings unless a specific exemption applies (e.g., proceedings before the Michigan Public Service Commission or the Employment Relations Commission are excepted).

> *Citation:* MI Admin Code R 792.10101 (Scope)  
> *Rule Set Title:* Michigan Administrative Hearing System Uniform Hearing Rules, Part 1  
> *Promulgated Under:* APA of 1969, PA 306  
> *Source (LII):* <https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/michigan/Mich-Admin-Code-R-792-10101>  
> *Full Rules PDF (LARA/ARS):* <https://ars.apps.lara.state.mi.us/AdminCode/DownloadAdminCodeFile?FileName=R%20792.10101%20to%20R%20792.11903.pdf>

---

### MAHS Uniform Hearing Rules — Part 10 (Public Benefits / MDHHS Scope)

**MI Admin Code R 792.11001** establishes the **scope** of the public-benefits hearing rules: these rules apply to administrative hearings conducted for the **Department of Human Services (DHS)** and the **Department of Community Health (DCH)** — now merged into the **Michigan Department of Health and Human Services (MDHHS)** — relating to benefits governed by the Social Welfare Act (1939 PA 280, MCL 400.1 to 400.122) and applicable federal regulations (Titles 7, 42, and 45 of the Code of Federal Regulations).

Covered programs include Medicaid, food assistance, cash assistance, and other public support programs administered by MDHHS.

> *Citation:* MI Admin Code R 792.11001 (Scope — Public Benefits)  
> *Rule Set Title:* Michigan Administrative Hearing System Uniform Hearing Rules, Part 10, Subpart A  
> *Source (Justia):* <https://regulations.justia.com/states/michigan/licensing-and-regulatory-affairs/michigan-administrative-hearing-system/michigan-administrative-hearing-system-uniform-hearing-rules/part-10/subpart-a/section-r-792-11001/>

---

### MDHHS Fair Hearing Pathway

When MDHHS makes an adverse decision on a benefit (e.g., denial, reduction, or termination of Medicaid or food assistance), the affected individual may request a **fair hearing** adjudicated by MOAHR's Benefit Services Division (BSD). The fair hearing is the administrative remedy that must generally be exhausted before judicial review in circuit court.

> *Hearing Request Information:* <https://www.michigan.gov/mdhhs/assistance-programs/medicaid/hearings>  
> *MOAHR Page for MDHHS:* <https://www.michigan.gov/mdhhs/assistance-programs/medicaid/hearings> (see also MOAHR main page: <https://www.michigan.gov/lara/bureau-list/moahr>)  
> *Governing Rules:* MI Admin Code R 792.11001 et seq.; APA of 1969 (PA 306)  
> *Judicial Review Authority:* MCL 24.301 et seq. (circuit court)  
> *Type:* Administrative Hearing Pathway (NOT a court proceeding)

## 5. Corporate/Business Law Coverage

This section maps key Michigan corporate and business litigation venues and authority sets relevant to business disputes.

---

### Michigan Business Court Program

Michigan circuit courts operate a **Business Court** program for complex business and commercial disputes. Business Courts are specialized dockets within the circuit court system, not separate courts.

**Jurisdiction and Eligibility (MCL 600.8035):**
- Business courts have authority over business and commercial disputes seeking equitable or declaratory relief, or monetary claims otherwise meeting the circuit court jurisdictional threshold (generally exceeding $25,000; *see* MCL 600.8301(1))
- Cases must involve a business or commercial dispute as defined under MCL 600.8031 et seq.
- Certain matters are **excluded** from business court jurisdiction under MCL 600.8031(3), including most employment actions and probate cases

> *Authority:* MCL 600.8035 (Business Court; Jurisdiction; Venue); MCL 600.8031 et seq.  
> *Source (Michigan Legislature):* <https://www.legislature.mi.gov/Laws/MCL?objectName=MCL-600-8035>  
> *SCAO Business Court Search:* <https://www.courts.michigan.gov/business-court-search/>  
> *Court Type:* Specialized docket within Circuit Court (general trial court tier)

### Venue for Business Disputes

Business disputes in Michigan are generally filed in **circuit court** (the court of general jurisdiction) or, where applicable, the **federal district courts** for federal claims or diversity jurisdiction. The Business Court program within specific circuit courts provides a specialized docket for qualifying matters.

> *Venue Authority:* MCL 600.1601 et seq. (general venue provisions)

## 6. Case Type Classification Codes

Every Michigan case number ends with a two-letter alpha code indicating the court's jurisdiction and the nature of the case. These codes are assigned by the State Court Administrative Office (SCAO).

---

### Circuit Court (Cir. Ct.)

*(See [§ 1 — Circuit Court](#cir-ct--circuit-court) for court description.)*

| Code | Label | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| **AA** | Administrative Agencies | Appeal | Appeals from decisions of state and local administrative agencies, including licensing boards and regulatory bodies. |
| **AR** | Criminal Appeals | Appeal | Appeals of criminal convictions or sentences from the district court to the circuit court. |
| **AV** | Civil Appeals | Appeal | Appeals of civil judgments from the district court or probate court to the circuit court. |
| **CH** | Housing | Civil | Circuit-level housing matters, including appeals from district court housing cases or circuit-jurisdiction housing issues. |
| **DM** | Divorce with Minor Children | Domestic Relations | Divorce or separate maintenance cases with minor children, requiring custody, parenting time, and child support determinations. |
| **FY** | Felony | Criminal | Criminal cases involving felony-level offenses. Circuit courts have original jurisdiction over all felony proceedings in Michigan. |

---

### District Court (Dist. Ct.)

*(See [§ 1 — District Court](#dist-ct--district-court) for court description and jurisdiction limit authority.)*

| Code | Label | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| **GC** | General Civil | Civil | General civil claims for money damages up to the jurisdictional limit of $25,000. *Authority: MCL 600.8301(1).* |
| **LT** | Landlord-Tenant | Civil | Summary proceedings for possession of residential or commercial property, including eviction proceedings. |
| **SC** | Small Claims | Civil | Informal proceedings for civil money claims. **Jurisdictional limit: $7,000 effective 2024-01-01** (MCL 600.8401(1)(e)). Attorneys generally may not represent parties. |
| **SM** | Statute Misdemeanor | Criminal | State-law misdemeanor offenses punishable by up to one year in jail. |
| **SI** | Statute Civil Infraction | Civil Infraction | Non-criminal civil infraction violations, including traffic offenses, where no jail time may be imposed. |

> *Small Claims Authority:* MCL 600.8401, Revised Judicature Act of 1961  
> *Small Claims Jurisdictional Limit Schedule (MCL 600.8401):*  
> — Beginning **2024-01-01**: $7,000.00 (current limit)  
> — 2021–2023: $6,500.00  
> — Prior periods: lower amounts set by statute (consult MCL 600.8401 for full schedule)  
> *Source:* <https://www.legislature.mi.gov/Laws/MCL?objectName=mcl-600-8401>

*(See [§ 1 — Probate Court](#prob-ct--probate-court) for court description.)*

| Code | Label | Type | Description |

## 7. Sourcing & Verification

### Evidence Standard

This catalogue applies an **authority-anchored** evidence standard: every factual assertion about jurisdiction, procedure, effective dates, or authority scope must be tied to an identified primary source (Michigan Legislature, Michigan Constitution, Michigan Supreme Court, SCAO, official federal court site, or equivalent). Quotations are reserved for items extracted verbatim from those sources.

### Treatment of UNVERIFIED Items

Items marked **[UNVERIFIED]** contain:
- A description of what could not be confirmed from available primary sources
- An **ACQUIRE PLAN** — the recommended source to consult to verify or correct the item

Consumers of this catalogue **must not rely on [UNVERIFIED] items as litigation-grade authority** without independent verification.
