## 1. Michigan State Court Abbreviations

Michigan's courts form the "One Court of Justice" system established by the Michigan Constitution of 1963, Article VI.

| Abbreviation | Full Name | Acronym | Tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mich.** | Michigan Supreme Court | MSC | Supreme Court |
| **Mich. App.** | Michigan Court of Appeals | COA | Appellate Court |
| **Cir. Ct.** | Circuit Court | — | Trial Court – General |
| **Dist. Ct.** | District Court | — | Trial Court – Limited |
| **Prob. Ct.** | Probate Court | — | Trial Court – Limited |
| **Mun. Ct.** | Municipal Court | — | Trial Court – Local |
| **JTC** | Judicial Tenure Commission | — | Administrative / Oversight Body |

### Mich. — Michigan Supreme Court (MSC)

The **Michigan Supreme Court** is the highest court in Michigan. It has superintending control over all courts in the state, possesses final appellate jurisdiction, and its opinions are cited using the reporter abbreviation **Mich.**

> *Constitutional Basis:* Mich. Const. 1963, art. VI, § 1

---

### Mich. App. — Michigan Court of Appeals (COA)

The **Michigan Court of Appeals** is the intermediate appellate court. It hears appeals from the circuit courts, the probate courts, and certain administrative agencies. Opinions are cited as **Mich. App.**

> *Constitutional Basis:* Mich. Const. 1963, art. VI, § 1

---

### Cir. Ct. — Circuit Court

The **Circuit Court** is the highest trial court in Michigan, with general original jurisdiction over:
- Civil claims exceeding $25,000
- Felony criminal cases
- Family law matters (divorce, custody, parenting time)
- Appeals from district and probate courts
- Appeals from certain administrative agencies

> *Constitutional Basis:* Mich. Const. 1963, art. VI, § 11

---

### Dist. Ct. — District Court

Often called the **"people's court"**, the District Court is the most frequently used court in Michigan. It handles:
- Civil claims up to $25,000
- Misdemeanor criminal offenses
- Civil infractions (including traffic tickets)
- Landlord-tenant disputes
- Small claims matters

> *Constitutional Basis:* Mich. Const. 1963, art. VI, § 15

---

### Prob. Ct. — Probate Court

The **Probate Court** has exclusive jurisdiction over:
- Administration of decedents' estates
- Trusts
- Mental health proceedings (involuntary hospitalization)
- Guardianships and conservatorships
- Matters involving minors

> *Constitutional Basis:* Mich. Const. 1963, art. VI, § 15

---

### Mun. Ct. — Municipal Court

**Municipal Courts** are retained by a few cities such as Grosse Pointe. These courts predate the district court system (established in 1968) and continue to operate under local authority, handling local ordinance violations and limited civil matters.

> *Note:* Most municipal courts were replaced by the district court system established in 1968.

---

### JTC — Judicial Tenure Commission

The **Judicial Tenure Commission** is an independent body that investigates complaints against judges in Michigan. The JTC has the authority to recommend discipline—including removal from office—to the Michigan Supreme Court.

> *Constitutional Basis:* Mich. Const. 1963, art. VI, § 30

---

### U.S. — United States Supreme Court

The **United States Supreme Court** is the highest court in the federal judiciary and the final authority on federal constitutional questions. Its decisions are binding on all courts throughout the United States.

> *Citation Example:* *Marbury v. Madison*, 5 U.S. 137 (1803)

---

### 6th Cir. — U.S. Court of Appeals for the Sixth Circuit

The **Sixth Circuit** is the federal intermediate appellate court covering **Michigan**, Ohio, Kentucky, and Tennessee. Its decisions are binding on all federal district courts within those four states.

> *Location:* Cincinnati, Ohio  
> *Citation Example:* *Smith v. Jones*, 123 F.3d 456 (6th Cir. 2020)

---

### E.D. Mich. — U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Michigan

The federal trial court for the **eastern portion of Michigan**, including Detroit, Flint, Ann Arbor, Saginaw, and surrounding areas.

> *Primary Courthouse:* Detroit, Michigan  
> *Citation Example:* *Doe v. Roe*, 456 F. Supp. 3d 789 (E.D. Mich. 2021)

---

### W.D. Mich. — U.S. District Court for the Western District of Michigan

The federal trial court for the **western portion of Michigan**, including Grand Rapids, Lansing, Kalamazoo, **Muskegon**, Marquette, and surrounding areas.

> *Primary Courthouse:* Grand Rapids, Michigan  
> *Division Locations:* Grand Rapids, Kalamazoo, Lansing, Marquette  
> *Notable Cities Covered:* Grand Rapids, Kalamazoo, Lansing, Muskegon, Marquette, Traverse City  
> *Citation Example:* *Doe v. Roe*, 789 F. Supp. 3d 123 (W.D. Mich. 2021)

## 3. Court Rule & Administrative Authorities

The primary rule sets and administrative bodies governing the Michigan "One Court of Justice" system.

| Abbreviation | Full Name | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| **MCR** | Michigan Court Rules | General procedure in all Michigan courts |
| **MRE** | Michigan Rules of Evidence | Evidentiary standards in all Michigan courts |
| **SCAO** | State Court Administrative Office | Statewide court administration and standardized forms |
| **MRPC** | Michigan Rules of Professional Conduct | Professional ethics for Michigan-licensed attorneys |
| **MCJC** | Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct | Professional ethics for Michigan judges |

### MCR — Michigan Court Rules

The **Michigan Court Rules** are the principal set of procedural rules governing practice and procedure in all Michigan courts. Promulgated by the Michigan Supreme Court, the MCR covers pleadings, motions, discovery, trials, and appeals.

> *Promulgated by:* Michigan Supreme Court  
> *Citation Example:* MCR 2.116 (motion for summary disposition)

---

### MRE — Michigan Rules of Evidence

The **Michigan Rules of Evidence** govern the admissibility of evidence in Michigan courts. Modeled in part on the Federal Rules of Evidence, the MRE controls what evidence may be presented to the trier of fact at trial.

> *Promulgated by:* Michigan Supreme Court  
> *Citation Example:* MRE 803 (hearsay exceptions)

---

### SCAO — State Court Administrative Office

The **State Court Administrative Office** is the administrative arm of the Michigan Supreme Court. It is responsible for:
- Providing administrative support to the Michigan court system
- Collecting and publishing court statistics
- Developing and maintaining standard court forms used statewide

> *Parent Body:* Michigan Supreme Court

---

### MRPC — Michigan Rules of Professional Conduct

The **Michigan Rules of Professional Conduct** are the ethical rules governing attorneys licensed in Michigan. Violations may result in discipline by the Attorney Grievance Commission and the Attorney Discipline Board.

> *Promulgated by:* Michigan Supreme Court  
> *Enforcement:* Attorney Grievance Commission / Attorney Discipline Board  
> *Citation Example:* MRPC 1.7 (conflict of interest)

---

### MCJC — Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct

The **Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct** sets ethical standards governing the conduct of all judges in Michigan, including standards for impartiality, integrity, and the avoidance of impropriety. It is enforced through the Judicial Tenure Commission (JTC).

> *Promulgated by:* Michigan Supreme Court  
> *Enforcement:* Judicial Tenure Commission (JTC)

## 5. Family Law & Fathers' Rights

### Child Custody Standards

Michigan employs two distinct standards depending on the procedural posture.

#### Best-Interest Factors (MCL 722.23)

Courts must consider and explicitly state findings on **all twelve factors**:

| Factor | Description |
|---|---|
| **(a)** | Love, affection, and other emotional ties between parties and child |
| **(b)** | Capacity and disposition to give child love, affection, and guidance |
| **(c)** | Capacity and disposition to provide material needs |
| **(d)** | Length of time child has lived in a stable, satisfactory environment |
| **(e)** | Permanence of family unit |
| **(f)** | Moral fitness of parties |
| **(g)** | Mental and physical health of parties |
| **(h)** | Home, school, and community record of child |
| **(i)** | Child's reasonable preference (if age/maturity appropriate) |
| **(j)** | Willingness of each parent to facilitate relationship with other parent |
| **(k)** | Domestic violence |
| **(l)** | Any other relevant factor |

> *Authority:* MCL 722.23; *Eldred v Ziny*, 246 Mich App 142 (2001)

#### Standard for Modification (MCL 722.27(1)(c))

To modify a custody order, the moving party must first establish:
1. **Proper cause** — a legally sufficient reason, or
2. **Change of circumstances** — a material change since the last order

Only after this threshold is met may the court conduct a best-interest analysis.

> *Authority:* MCL 722.27(1)(c); *Vodvarka v Grasmeyer*, 259 Mich App 499 (2003)

---

### Parenting Time

**MCL 722.27a** establishes that parenting time is the child's right, not simply the parent's right.

Key principles:
- Parenting time shall be granted in a frequency, duration, and type reasonably calculated to promote a strong relationship
- Neither parent may unilaterally deny the other's parenting time
- Makeup time is a remedy for improperly denied parenting time
- The court may impose conditions: drug testing, supervision, exchange procedures

**Enforcement Mechanisms:**
1. Contempt of court (MCR 3.208; MCL 552.641)
2. Show cause / bench warrant
3. Makeup parenting time order
4. Modification of custody as sanction for willful denial
5. FOC enforcement complaint

> *Authority:* MCL 722.27a; *Rivette v Rose-Molina*, 278 Mich App 327 (2008)

---

### Child Support

Michigan uses the **Michigan Child Support Formula (MCSF)**, promulgated by the Michigan Supreme Court and updated periodically. The formula is presumptively correct.

Key components:
- **Base support** — calculated from net incomes of both parents and overnight percentage
- **Medical support** — health insurance and uninsured medical expense sharing
- **Child care support** — work/school-related child care costs
- **Deviation** — court may deviate from MCSF if application would be unjust or inappropriate; *must* state findings

**Income Withholding (IWO):** All Michigan support orders contain an automatic income withholding order unless the court finds good cause to deviate.

**Imputed Income:** If a parent is voluntarily unemployed or underemployed, the court may impute income at the parent's potential earning capacity.

> *Authority:* MCL 552.519; MCL 552.605; MCSF Manual (current edition); *Ghidotti v Barber*, 459 Mich 189 (1998)

## 6. Friend of the Court (FOC)

---

### FOC Jurisdiction & Duties

The **Friend of the Court** is an arm of the circuit court's family division operating under the Friend of the Court Act (MCL 552.501 et seq.). Every county has an FOC office.

**Core Functions:**
- Investigate and make recommendations on custody, parenting time, and support
- Monitor and enforce support orders (income withholding, license suspension, contempt referrals)
- Conduct mediation between parties (MCL 552.513)
- Maintain records of support payments through the Michigan State Disbursement Unit (MiSDU)
- Investigate parenting time complaints

**Grievance Procedure (MCL 552.501(6)):**
Any party may file a written grievance with the FOC office about the performance of its duties. If unresolved, the party may petition the circuit court for review.

**Opting Out / Waiver (MCL 552.505a):**
Parties may jointly request FOC services be waived if: (1) no minor children are involved, or (2) both parties agree and the court approves. Domestic violence history may preclude waiver.

## 7. Judicial Disqualification & Judicial Conduct

---

### MCR 2.003 — Disqualification of Judge

**MCR 2.003** is the primary procedural rule for seeking disqualification of a judge in Michigan.

#### Grounds for Disqualification (MCR 2.003(C))

A judge **must** be disqualified if:

| Ground | Description |
|---|---|
| **(1) Personal bias or prejudice** | The judge has actual personal bias or prejudice for or against a party or attorney |
| **(2) Personal knowledge** | The judge has personal knowledge of disputed evidentiary facts |
| **(3) Former involvement** | The judge was a material witness, attorney, or party in the proceeding |
| **(4) Financial interest** | The judge has a financial interest in the subject matter of the proceeding |
| **(5) Relationship** | The judge or a family member has a relationship with a party or attorney creating a conflict |
| **(6) Impartiality reasonably questioned** | The judge's impartiality might reasonably be questioned by an objective, disinterested observer |
| **(7) Campaign statements** | The judge made an unconditional promise or commitment about an issue in the case during a campaign |

#### Procedure (MCR 2.003(D))

1. **Party files motion** with the challenged judge stating specific facts and grounds
2. **Judge rules on own motion** — may grant or deny
3. **If denied** — party files motion for review with the chief judge (or random assignment panel)
4. **Chief judge reviews** — upholds or overrules the denial
5. **Immediate appeal** — a party may seek leave to appeal to the Court of Appeals; this does not stay proceedings unless ordered

**Timing:** Motion must be filed promptly after the basis for disqualification is discovered. Delay may constitute waiver.

> *Authority:* MCR 2.003; *Cain v Dep't of Corrections*, 451 Mich 470 (1996); *In re MKK*, 286 Mich App 546 (2009)

---

### MCJC Canons

The **Michigan Code of Judicial Conduct** (MCJC) is enforced by the **Judicial Tenure Commission** (JTC). Key canons:

| Canon | Title | Core Principle |
|---|---|---|
| **Canon 1** | Upholding the Integrity and Independence of the Judiciary | A judge shall participate in establishing, maintaining, and enforcing high standards of conduct |
| **Canon 2** | Avoiding Impropriety and the Appearance of Impropriety | A judge shall respect and comply with the law and shall act at all times in a manner that promotes public confidence |
| **Canon 2A** | Impartiality | A judge shall be faithful to the law and maintain professional competence in it |
| **Canon 2B** | Non-Use of Prestige | A judge shall not allow family or other relationships to influence the judge's judicial conduct |
| **Canon 3** | Performing the Duties of the Office Impartially and Diligently | Governs hearings, disposition of matters, disqualification |
| **Canon 3A(4)** | Patient and Dignified Treatment | A judge shall be patient, dignified, and courteous to all who appear |
| **Canon 3A(6)** | Exclusion of Ex Parte Communications | A judge shall not initiate, permit, or consider ex parte communications concerning a pending proceeding |
| **Canon 3C** | Disqualification | Same grounds as MCR 2.003; judge has independent duty to recuse |
| **Canon 3D** | Remittal of Disqualification | Parties may consent to proceed despite a disqualification ground |
| **Canon 5** | Engaging in Extra-Judicial Activities | Limitations on outside employment and activities |
| **Canon 7** | Political Activity | Restrictions on judges and candidates for judicial office |

**Canon 3A(6) — Ex Parte Communications:**  
A judge must not communicate privately with a party or counsel about a pending matter without the opposing party's knowledge. Violation is grounds for JTC complaint and may require reversal.

### Complaint Pathway

1. **File JTC complaint** — Judicial Tenure Commission, 3034 W. Grand Blvd., Suite 8-450, Detroit, MI 48202
   - Form: JTC-1 (available at https://www.michigan.gov/jtc)
   - No filing fee; complaint is confidential during investigation
2. **JTC investigates** — may dismiss, issue caution, or file formal complaint
3. **Formal hearing** — before a JTC master; record made
4. **JTC recommendation** — transmitted to Michigan Supreme Court
5. **Michigan Supreme Court** — final order (censure, suspension, removal, or retirement)

> *Note:* A pending JTC complaint does **not** automatically disqualify the judge or stay the case. A separate MCR 2.003 motion must be filed to disqualify the judge from a specific case.

## 8. Due Process Checklist

---

### Constitutional Bases

| Authority | Provision | Application |
|---|---|---|
| **U.S. Const. amend. XIV, § 1** | "…nor shall any State deprive any person of life, liberty, or property, without due process of law" | Federal floor; applies to all state court proceedings |
| **Mich. Const. 1963, art. I, § 17** | "No person shall be…deprived of life, liberty or property, without due process of law" | Michigan counterpart; generally coextensive with federal |
| **Mich. Const. 1963, art. I, § 20** | Rights of accused | Applies in criminal and quasi-criminal proceedings |

**Parental rights are a constitutionally protected liberty interest** under both the federal and Michigan constitutions. The government may not terminate, restrict, or alter parental rights without procedural due process.

> *Authority:* *Troxel v Granville*, 530 US 57 (2000); *Stanley v Illinois*, 405 US 645 (1972); *In re Clausen*, 442 Mich 648 (1993)

---

### Mathews v. Eldridge Balancing Test

Federal and Michigan courts apply the *Mathews v Eldridge* three-factor balancing test to determine what process is due:

| Factor | Description |
|---|---|
| **(1) Private interest** | The importance of the private interest that will be affected by the official action |
| **(2) Risk of erroneous deprivation** | The risk of an erroneous deprivation of that interest through the procedures used, and the value of additional safeguards |
| **(3) Government interest** | The government's interest, including the fiscal and administrative burdens of additional procedural requirements |

> *Authority:* *Mathews v Eldridge*, 424 US 319 (1976); *Turner v Rogers*, 564 US 431 (2011) (applying *Mathews* to civil contempt in child support); *In re Rood*, 483 Mich 73 (2009)

---

### Procedural Checklist for Family Law Hearings

Use this checklist to identify and preserve due process objections:

**Before the Hearing:**
- [ ] Adequate notice of hearing provided (MCR 2.119; MCR 3.206)
- [ ] Notice includes specific issues to be decided
- [ ] Sufficient time to prepare (MCR 2.108; MCR 2.119(C))
- [ ] All evidence to be offered has been disclosed
- [ ] Opportunity to conduct discovery (MCR 2.301 et seq.)
- [ ] No ex parte communications with the court (MCJC Canon 3A(6))

**At the Hearing:**
- [ ] Party has right to be present and heard
- [ ] Party has opportunity to present evidence and witnesses
- [ ] Party has opportunity to cross-examine adverse witnesses
- [ ] Hearsay objections preserved if improper hearsay admitted
- [ ] FOC/referee report objections preserved (MCR 3.210; FOC 23/24)
- [ ] Judge has disclosed any disqualification grounds (MCR 2.003)

**The Decision:**
- [ ] Court makes findings of fact on the record or in writing
- [ ] Court addresses all required statutory factors (e.g., all 12 MCL 722.23 factors in custody)
- [ ] Court states reasons for deviation from guidelines (if applicable)
- [ ] Order is consistent with the findings

**After the Hearing:**
- [ ] Objection to referee recommendation filed within 21 days (MCR 3.207; FOC 23)
- [ ] Motion for reconsideration filed within 21 days if appropriate (MCR 2.119(F))
- [ ] Notice of appeal filed within 21 days for final orders (MCR 7.204(A)(1))
- [ ] Transcript ordered to preserve record for appeal (MCR 7.210)

> *Failure to object at the hearing generally waives the issue on appeal.* See *Walters v Nadell*, 481 Mich 377 (2008).

### Issue Preservation Rules

To preserve an issue for appeal, a party **must** raise it in the trial court first. Failure to object = waiver.

| Issue Type | How to Preserve |
|---|---|
| **Evidentiary objection** | Object at the time evidence is offered; state the specific ground (MRE 103) |
| **Constitutional claim** | Raise in trial court; general objection insufficient |
| **Sufficiency of findings** | Object on the record that court has not addressed required factors |
| **Referee recommendation** | File written objection within 21 days (FOC 23/24) |
| **Disqualification** | File MCR 2.003 motion promptly upon discovering ground |
| **PPO challenge** | File motion to modify/terminate within 14 days (MCR 3.703(G)) |
| **Contempt ability-to-pay** | Assert inability to pay at the contempt hearing; present evidence |
| **Hearsay** | Specific objection identifying the statement and hearsay ground (MRE 802) |

> *Authority:* *Walters v Nadell*, 481 Mich 377 (2008) (unpreserved error reviewed for plain error only); MCR 2.517(A)(7) (duty to request findings).

**Plain Error Standard:**
Unpreserved errors are reviewed for plain error affecting substantial rights:
1. Error occurred
2. Error was plain (clear or obvious)
3. Error affected substantial rights (outcome determinative)
4. Manifest injustice requires reversal

> *Authority:* *People v Carines*, 460 Mich 750 (1999) (criminal); *Vushaj v Farm Bureau Gen Ins Co*, 284 Mich App 513 (2009) (civil).

## 12. PPO Defense Toolkit

---

### Types of PPOs

| Type | Statute | Who Can Petition | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Domestic Relationship PPO** | MCL 600.2950 | Spouse, former spouse, person with child in common, dating relationship, person residing/formerly residing | Assault, threatening conduct, stalking, entry into residence |
| **Non-Domestic (Stalking) PPO** | MCL 600.2950a | Any person | Stalking (MCL 750.411h); aggravated stalking (MCL 750.411i) |
| **Sexual Assault PPO** | MCL 600.2950a | Any person who was sexually assaulted | Specific to sexual assault respondents |

---

### Ex Parte PPO — Motion to Terminate

An **ex parte PPO** is issued without notice to the respondent. Upon being served, the respondent has the right to challenge it.

**Procedure:**
1. Respondent files **Motion to Modify or Terminate PPO** (CC 380) in the issuing court
2. Motion must be filed **within 14 days of service** — this is a hard deadline (MCR 3.703(G))
3. Court **must** schedule a hearing within 14 days of the motion
4. At hearing, **petitioner bears burden** to justify continuation of PPO

> *If respondent misses the 14-day deadline, the PPO becomes permanent absent exceptional circumstances.*

---

### Hearing Rights

At the PPO hearing, the respondent has the right to:
- [ ] Be present
- [ ] Present evidence
- [ ] Cross-examine the petitioner and any witnesses
- [ ] Contest the factual basis for the PPO
- [ ] Challenge the petitioner's standing (domestic relationship)

---

### Grounds to Challenge

| Ground | Argument |
|---|---|
| **No domestic relationship** | MCL 600.2950 requires a qualifying relationship; challenge petitioner's standing |
| **Insufficient factual basis** | Vague, generalized fear without specific acts; *Pickering v Pickering*, 253 Mich App 694 |
| **No reasonable cause** | Petitioner cannot show reasonable cause to believe respondent will commit prohibited act |
| **False statements** | If petitioner made material misrepresentations, move to terminate and consider perjury referral |
| **First Amendment** | Constitutionally protected speech (political, religious) cannot be prohibited by PPO |
| **Due process** | If ex parte order was issued without the statutory predicate of irreparable harm (MCR 3.703(B)) |
| **Stalking elements not met** | For non-domestic PPO: course of conduct must constitute stalking under MCL 750.411h |

---

### Violation Defense

If charged with PPO violation (criminal contempt, MCL 764.15b; or civil contempt):

| Defense | Basis |
|---|---|
| **Lack of notice/service** | PPO must be personally served to be enforceable; constructive notice insufficient for criminal contempt |
| **No willful violation** | Unintentional contact (coincidental meeting) is not a willful violation |
| **Petitioner initiated contact** | Petitioner's own contact with respondent does not waive PPO but is relevant to scienter |
| **Modification pending** | Note: pending motion to modify does not stay the PPO |
| **Ambiguous order** | PPO language too vague to give fair notice of prohibited conduct |
| **Inability to pay (civil contempt)** | *Turner v Rogers*, 564 US 431; *Tkachik v Mandeville*, 487 Mich 38 |

> *Authority:* MCR 3.707; MCL 764.15b; *People v Pickering*, 2011 WL XXXXXX (Mich App) [UNVERIFIED]

### [UNVERIFIED] Labeling Policy

Throughout this catalogue, citations and holdings marked **[UNVERIFIED]** indicate one or more of the following conditions:

1. **Citation not independently confirmed** — The case name, reporter volume, page, or year has not been confirmed against an official reporter or authenticated legal database.
2. **Holding paraphrased** — The description of the holding is a paraphrase or summary that may not precisely reflect the court's language.
3. **Unpublished / non-precedential** — The case is an unpublished opinion of the Michigan Court of Appeals and is therefore not precedentially binding under MCR 7.215(C)(1).
4. **No docket number verified** — The case number or docket number has not been confirmed.

**Users of this catalogue should independently verify all [UNVERIFIED] citations through Westlaw, LexisNexis, or the official Michigan reporter before relying on them in court filings or legal argument.**

All verified citations have been cross-checked against published reporters or authenticated online databases. Statutes and court rules are based on current MCL and MCR texts as of the date of last revision.
