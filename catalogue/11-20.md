### Issue Preservation Rules

To preserve an issue for appeal, a party **must** raise it in the trial court first. Failure to object = waiver.

| Issue Type | How to Preserve |
|---|---|
| **Evidentiary objection** | Object at the time evidence is offered; state the specific ground (MRE 103) |
| **Constitutional claim** | Raise in trial court; general objection insufficient |
| **Sufficiency of findings** | Object on the record that court has not addressed required factors |
| **Referee recommendation** | File written objection within 21 days (FOC 23/24) |
| **Disqualification** | File MCR 2.003 motion promptly upon discovering ground |
| **PPO challenge** | File motion to modify/terminate within 14 days (MCR 3.703(G)) |
| **Contempt ability-to-pay** | Assert inability to pay at the contempt hearing; present evidence |
| **Hearsay** | Specific objection identifying the statement and hearsay ground (MRE 802) |

> *Authority:* *Walters v Nadell*, 481 Mich 377 (2008) (unpreserved error reviewed for plain error only); MCR 2.517(A)(7) (duty to request findings).

**Plain Error Standard:**
Unpreserved errors are reviewed for plain error affecting substantial rights:
1. Error occurred
2. Error was plain (clear or obvious)
3. Error affected substantial rights (outcome determinative)
4. Manifest injustice requires reversal

> *Authority:* *People v Carines*, 460 Mich 750 (1999) (criminal); *Vushaj v Farm Bureau Gen Ins Co*, 284 Mich App 513 (2009) (civil).

## 12. PPO Defense Toolkit


---

### Types of PPOs

| Type | Statute | Who Can Petition | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Domestic Relationship PPO** | MCL 600.2950 | Spouse, former spouse, person with child in common, dating relationship, person residing/formerly residing | Assault, threatening conduct, stalking, entry into residence |
| **Non-Domestic (Stalking) PPO** | MCL 600.2950a | Any person | Stalking (MCL 750.411h); aggravated stalking (MCL 750.411i) |
| **Sexual Assault PPO** | MCL 600.2950a | Any person who was sexually assaulted | Specific to sexual assault respondents |

---

### Ex Parte PPO — Motion to Terminate

An **ex parte PPO** is issued without notice to the respondent. Upon being served, the respondent has the right to challenge it.

**Procedure:**
1. Respondent files **Motion to Modify or Terminate PPO** (CC 380) in the issuing court
2. Motion must be filed **within 14 days of service** — this is a hard deadline (MCR 3.703(G))
3. Court **must** schedule a hearing within 14 days of the motion
4. At hearing, **petitioner bears burden** to justify continuation of PPO

> *If respondent misses the 14-day deadline, the PPO becomes permanent absent exceptional circumstances.*

---

### Hearing Rights

At the PPO hearing, the respondent has the right to:
- [ ] Be present
- [ ] Present evidence
- [ ] Cross-examine the petitioner and any witnesses
- [ ] Contest the factual basis for the PPO
- [ ] Challenge the petitioner's standing (domestic relationship)

---

### Grounds to Challenge

| Ground | Argument |
|---|---|
| **No domestic relationship** | MCL 600.2950 requires a qualifying relationship; challenge petitioner's standing |
| **Insufficient factual basis** | Vague, generalized fear without specific acts; *Pickering v Pickering*, 253 Mich App 694 |
| **No reasonable cause** | Petitioner cannot show reasonable cause to believe respondent will commit prohibited act |
| **False statements** | If petitioner made material misrepresentations, move to terminate and consider perjury referral |
| **First Amendment** | Constitutionally protected speech (political, religious) cannot be prohibited by PPO |
| **Due process** | If ex parte order was issued without the statutory predicate of irreparable harm (MCR 3.703(B)) |
| **Stalking elements not met** | For non-domestic PPO: course of conduct must constitute stalking under MCL 750.411h |

---

### Violation Defense

If charged with PPO violation (criminal contempt, MCL 764.15b; or civil contempt):

| Defense | Basis |
|---|---|
| **Lack of notice/service** | PPO must be personally served to be enforceable; constructive notice insufficient for criminal contempt |
| **No willful violation** | Unintentional contact (coincidental meeting) is not a willful violation |
| **Petitioner initiated contact** | Petitioner's own contact with respondent does not waive PPO but is relevant to scienter |
| **Modification pending** | Note: pending motion to modify does not stay the PPO |
| **Ambiguous order** | PPO language too vague to give fair notice of prohibited conduct |
| **Inability to pay (civil contempt)** | *Turner v Rogers*, 564 US 431; *Tkachik v Mandeville*, 487 Mich 38 |

> *Authority:* MCR 3.707; MCL 764.15b; *People v Pickering*, 2011 WL XXXXXX (Mich App) [UNVERIFIED]

---

### MCL 600.2950 — Prohibited Acts (Domestic Relationship PPO)

A domestic relationship PPO may prohibit any of the following acts (MCL 600.2950(1)):

| Act | Description |
|---|---|
| **(a)** | Entering onto premises |
| **(b)** | Assaulting, attacking, beating, molesting, or wounding the petitioner |
| **(c)** | Threatening to kill or physically injure the petitioner or another person |
| **(d)** | Removing minor children from the person having legal custody, except as authorized by existing custody order |
| **(e)** | Stalking as defined in MCL 750.411h or 750.411i |
| **(f)** | Purchasing or possessing a firearm |
| **(g)** | Interfering with petitioner at petitioner's place of employment or education; interfering with petitioner's employment or educational relationships or environment |
| **(h)** | Having access to information in the records of the department regarding address, employer, or school of petitioner or minor children |
| **(i)** | Engaging in conduct that imposes on or interferes with personal liberty or that causes a reasonable apprehension of violence |
| **(j)** | Posting a message through the use of any medium of communication, including the internet or a computer or any electronic medium |

**Important:** The order must specify which prohibited acts apply. A blanket "all of the above" order may be challenged on specificity grounds.

---

### MCL 600.2950a — Non-Domestic PPO — Prohibited Acts

A non-domestic stalking PPO may prohibit the same acts listed in MCL 600.2950(1)(a)–(j) above and additionally:
- Following petitioner to or from work
- Appearing at petitioner's home or workplace
- Monitoring petitioner's internet or electronic communications
- Sending petitioner electronic communications or messages

---

### Stalking — MCL 750.411h — Elements Atomized

For a **non-domestic stalking PPO** under MCL 600.2950a, petitioner must show conduct constituting **stalking** (MCL 750.411h) or **aggravated stalking** (MCL 750.411i).

**Stalking — MCL 750.411h(1):**
"Stalking" means a **willful course of conduct** involving **repeated or continuing harassment** of an individual that would cause a **reasonable person** to feel terrorized, frightened, intimidated, threatened, harassed, or molested, and that actually causes the victim to feel terrorized, frightened, intimidated, threatened, harassed, or molested.

**Required Elements:**
1. **Willful** — the conduct is intentional
2. **Course of conduct** — a pattern of behavior composed of 2 or more separate noncontinuous acts evidencing a continuity of purpose
3. **Repeated or continuing harassment** — the conduct must be more than an isolated act
4. **Objectively reasonable person** would feel terrorized, frightened, intimidated, threatened, harassed, or molested
5. **Subjectively** — the petitioner actually feels terrorized, frightened, intimidated, threatened, harassed, or molested

**Harassment — MCL 750.411h(1)(c):**
"Harassment" means conduct directed toward a victim that includes, but is not limited to, **repeated or continuing** unconsented contact that would cause a reasonable individual to suffer emotional distress.

**Aggravated Stalking — MCL 750.411i:**
Adds one or more aggravating circumstances:
- Violation of a PPO or restraining order
- Making a credible threat (express or implicit)
- Prior stalking conviction
- Victim is under 18
- Defendant was armed with a dangerous weapon

## 13. Benchbooks & Citation Format

### Michigan Citation Format

Michigan uses the **Uniform System of Citation** with local modifications. The authoritative guide is the **Michigan Uniform System of Citation** published by the Michigan Supreme Court and updated periodically.

#### Cases

| Court | Format | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Michigan Supreme Court | *Party v Party*, [vol] Mich [page] ([year]) | *Fletcher v Fletcher*, 447 Mich 871 (1994) |
| Michigan Court of Appeals (published) | *Party v Party*, [vol] Mich App [page] ([year]) | *Vodvarka v Grasmeyer*, 259 Mich App 499 (2003) |
| Michigan Court of Appeals (unpublished) | *Party v Party*, unpublished per curiam opinion of the Court of Appeals, issued [date] (Docket No. XXXXXX) | — |
| U.S. Supreme Court | *Party v Party*, [vol] US [page] ([year]) | *Troxel v Granville*, 530 US 57 (2000) |
| 6th Circuit | *Party v Party*, [vol] F3d [page] (6th Cir [year]) | *Kottmyer v Maas*, 436 F3d 684 (6th Cir 2006) |

> **Unpublished opinions** of the Michigan Court of Appeals are not precedentially binding under MCR 7.215(C)(1) but may be cited for persuasive value with the notation "unpub op."

#### Statutes & Rules

| Authority | Format | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Michigan Compiled Laws | MCL [chapter].[section] | MCL 722.23 |
| Michigan Court Rules | MCR [chapter].[rule] | MCR 2.003(C)(6) |
| Michigan Rules of Evidence | MRE [rule number] | MRE 803(3) |
| Michigan Constitution | Mich Const 1963, art [X], § [Y] | Mich Const 1963, art I, § 17 |
| U.S. Constitution | US Const, amend [X] | US Const, amend XIV |

### Judicial Review of Agency Decisions

**Procedure:**
1. Party must **exhaust administrative remedies** before seeking judicial review
2. File **complaint for judicial review** in circuit court of county where agency decision issued **within 60 days** of final agency order (MCL 24.301–24.304)
3. Court reviews the **administrative record** — no new evidence unless record is insufficient
4. **Standard:** Substantial evidence on the whole record; agency findings of fact affirmed if supported by competent, material, and substantial evidence; questions of law reviewed de novo

**Key Cases:**

| Case | Citation | Holding |
|---|---|---|
| *Const 1963, art VI, § 28* | — | Constitutional right to judicial review of agency decisions in Michigan |
| *In re Complaint of Rovas Against SBC Michigan* | 482 Mich 90 (2008) | Agencies have no authority beyond powers delegated by legislature; ultra vires acts void |
| *Verizon North, Inc v Pub Serv Comm* | 263 Mich App 567 (2004) | Substantial evidence standard; court may not reweigh evidence |
| *Citizens Ins Co v Dep't of Ins & Financial Services* | 332 Mich App 338 (2020) | Agency interpretation of its enabling statute entitled to respectful consideration but not binding deference; court decides questions of law de novo |

> *Note:* Michigan does not follow *Chevron* deference — the Michigan Supreme Court has rejected mandatory deference to agency statutory interpretations. *See* *Dep't of Environmental Quality v Gomez*, unpub, and *In re Rovas*.

### MAPA Contested Case Procedure — Atomized (MCL 24.271–24.292)

**Step-by-Step Procedure:**

1. **Notice of Hearing (MCL 24.271)**
   - Agency must give reasonable notice of hearing to affected parties
   - Notice must include: time, place, nature of hearing, legal authority, issues to be heard
   - Must be given sufficiently in advance to allow preparation

2. **Hearing Rights (MCL 24.272)**
   - All parties have right to: submit evidence; cross-examine witnesses; make arguments; file objections
   - Informal disposition by consent; settlement; stipulation of facts (MCL 24.273)
   - Evidence: rules of evidence inapplicable unless agency adopts them; hearsay admissible if reliable; weight in agency discretion

3. **Subpoenas (MCL 24.277)**
   - ALJ or presiding officer may issue subpoenas for witnesses and documents
   - Subpoenas enforceable in circuit court

4. **Proposed Decision (MCL 24.281)**
   - After hearing: ALJ issues proposal for decision
   - Parties may file **exceptions** within time set by agency (usually 20–30 days)
   - Exceptions: must identify specific findings or conclusions objected to; cite evidence

5. **Final Decision (MCL 24.285)**
   - Agency issues final order after considering exceptions
   - Must contain: findings of fact on each contested material fact; conclusions of law; reasons
   - Must be served on all parties

6. **Judicial Review (MCL 24.301–24.306)**
   - File **complaint for review** in circuit court of county where agency's principal office is located
   - Deadline: **60 days** after final agency decision (jurisdictional)
   - Court reviews administrative record; may order additional evidence if record insufficient
   - Standards per MCL 24.306 (substantial evidence; arbitrary and capricious; beyond authority)

### MCL 712A — Jurisdiction & Grounds

The **juvenile code** (MCL 712A.1 et seq.) governs the family division of circuit court's jurisdiction over minors.

**Jurisdiction grounds (MCL 712A.2):**

| Subsection | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **(a)** | Delinquency | Minor alleged to have violated state law or local ordinance |
| **(b)** | Neglect/Abuse | Minor whose parent/guardian neglects or abuses the child; minor whose home is unfit; minor at substantial risk of harm |
| **(d)** | Status offenses | Minor who is absent from school, beyond parental control, or runaway |
| **(h)** | Child support | Enforcement against non-custodial parent through family division |

**Abuse/Neglect Petition (MCL 712A.2(b)):**

The court acquires jurisdiction if the minor's parent or guardian:
- Abandoned the child
- Subjected child to physical, mental, or emotional abuse or neglect
- Failed to provide proper care or custody
- Placed child at substantial risk of harm
- Committed or permitted sexual abuse

### Child Protective Services (CPS) Process

| Stage | Timeframe | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Initial Assessment** | 24 hrs (emergency) / 72 hrs (priority) | CPS worker contacts family; assesses immediate safety |
| **Safety Plan** | Immediate | Voluntary agreement to protect child during investigation |
| **Investigation** | 30 days | CPS determines whether abuse/neglect is substantiated |
| **Case Finding** | End of investigation | Substantiated (supports a finding) or Unsubstantiated (does not support) |

### Termination of Parental Rights (TPR)

**Grounds for TPR (MCL 712A.19b(3)):** The court may terminate parental rights if it finds by **clear and convincing evidence** that one or more statutory grounds are established **and** that termination is in the child's best interests (preponderance of evidence).

**Selected Statutory Grounds:**

| Subsection | Ground | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **(b)(i)** | Physical injury | Parent physically abused the child; likelihood of future injury |
| **(b)(ii)** | Sexual abuse | Parent sexually abused the child; likelihood of future abuse |
| **(g)** | Failure to provide care | Parent, without regard to intent, fails to provide proper care; no reasonable expectation of rectification |
| **(j)** | Reasonable likelihood of harm | Reasonable likelihood of harm if child returned to parent |
| **(k)** | Aggravated circumstances | Murder, manslaughter, or criminal assault of a child; denial of essential food/clothing/shelter |
| **(l)** | Prior TPR | Parent's rights to another child were previously terminated |
| **(m)** | Conviction | Parent convicted of certain crimes (e.g., first-degree murder, first-degree CSC involving minor) |

**Best Interests Analysis:**

After statutory grounds are established, the court **must** separately determine whether TPR is in the child's best interests. Factors include:
- The child's bond with the parent
- The child's need for permanency, stability, and finality
- The adequacy of the parent's home and parenting ability
- The child's well-being while in care

> *Authority:* MCL 712A.19b(5); *In re Moss*, 301 Mich App 76 (2013); *In re White*, 303 Mich App 701 (2014)

**Standard of Review on Appeal:**
- Statutory grounds: whether the trial court's findings of fact are clearly erroneous (*In re BZ*, 264 Mich App 286 (2004))
- Best interests: clearly erroneous (*In re Olive/Metts Minors*, 297 Mich App 35 (2012))

**Appeal:**
- Appeal of TPR order: claim of appeal to Court of Appeals within **21 days** (MCR 7.204(A)(1))
- Expedited briefing schedule applies

### Indian Child Welfare Act (ICWA)

The **Indian Child Welfare Act** (ICWA), 25 USC 1901 et seq., applies whenever a child who is an **Indian child** (member of, or eligible for membership in, a federally recognized tribe) is the subject of child custody proceedings.

| Requirement | Standard | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Tribal notice | Must notify tribe of any foster care or TPR proceeding | 25 USC 1912(a) |
| Active efforts | Active efforts to prevent removal and provide services | 25 USC 1912(d); *higher than "reasonable efforts"* |
| Foster care standard | Beyond a reasonable doubt that removal is necessary to prevent serious emotional or physical damage | 25 USC 1912(f) |
| TPR standard | Beyond a reasonable doubt (higher than state standard) | 25 USC 1912(f) |
| Placement preference | Indian family; extended family; tribally licensed home; Indian foster home | 25 USC 1915 |
| Tribal intervention | Tribe has right to intervene at any point | 25 USC 1911(c) |

> *ICWA compliance is mandatory. Failure to provide proper tribal notice is grounds for reversal.*

### Preliminary Hearing (MCR 3.965) — Atomized Procedure

The preliminary hearing must occur **within 24 hours or next business day** after a child is removed from the home without a court order.

**At the Preliminary Hearing, Court Must Determine:**
1. **Jurisdiction:** Whether the court has jurisdiction over the child
2. **Probable cause:** Whether there is probable cause to believe the child is within the court's jurisdiction under MCL 712A.2
3. **Safety:** Whether it is contrary to the welfare of the child to remain in or be returned to the home
4. **Placement:** Where the child should be placed pending further proceedings
5. **Reasonable efforts:** Whether the agency has made reasonable efforts to prevent removal or whether an emergency existed precluding such efforts

**Placement Preferences:**
1. With a relative (MCL 712A.13a(1)) — preferred
2. With licensed foster family
3. Group home
4. Residential facility (only if less restrictive placements inadequate)

**Orders the Court May Make:**
- Return child to parent with or without conditions
- Place child with relative under supervision
- Place child in licensed foster care
- Order services for parent; drug testing; parenting classes
- Authorize MDHHS placement

---

### Reasonable Efforts vs. Active Efforts (ICWA)

| Standard | Context | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Reasonable efforts** | Non-ICWA cases; reunification | Agency must make reasonable efforts to preserve family and prevent removal; reasonable efforts to reunify after removal (MCL 712A.19a; 42 USC 671(a)(15)) |
| **Active efforts** | ICWA cases | Higher standard; affirmative, active efforts to provide services; must go beyond merely offering services — must actively assist the parent in obtaining them (25 USC 1912(d); 25 CFR 23.2) |

**Active Efforts Definition (25 CFR 23.2):**
"Active efforts" means affirmative, active, thorough, and timely efforts intended primarily to maintain or reunite an Indian child with his or her family. Passive efforts — such as merely informing a parent of services — are insufficient.

| **OV (Offense Variables)** | Score the severity of the offense conduct (OV 1–OV 19) |
| **PRV (Prior Record Variables)** | Score the offender's criminal history (PRV 1–PRV 7) |
| **Guidelines Range** | OV + PRV scores place offense in a cell with minimum sentence range |
| **Minimum Sentence** | Court must impose minimum within guidelines range unless substantial and compelling reasons to depart |
| **Departure** | Court may depart with substantial and compelling reasons stated on record; subject to appellate review |
| **Consecutive Sentencing** | Some offenses require or permit consecutive sentences (MCL 768.7b) |
| **Presentence Investigation Report (PSI)** | Required before sentencing on any offense punishable by imprisonment; party may contest inaccuracies |

> *After* People v Lockridge, 498 Mich 358 (2015)*, the guidelines are advisory only following the holding that mandatory guidelines violated the Sixth Amendment. Courts must consult but are not bound by the range; any sentence within the statutory maximum may be imposed with adequate explanation.*

### Brady/Giglio Material — Discovery Rights

**Brady v Maryland**, 373 US 83 (1963): Prosecution must disclose all material exculpatory evidence.

**Giglio v United States**, 405 US 150 (1972): Duty extends to impeachment evidence about prosecution witnesses (prior convictions, deals with prosecution, inconsistent statements).

**Materiality Standard:** Evidence is material if there is a "reasonable probability" that disclosure would produce a different result.

**MCR 6.201 — Michigan Discovery Obligations:**
- Prosecution must disclose: names and addresses of witnesses; prior criminal convictions of prosecution witnesses; exculpatory evidence; any deal or agreement with a prosecution witness
- Defense must disclose: names of alibi witnesses; expert witnesses and their reports

**Brady Violation — Elements:**
1. The evidence is favorable to the defendant (exculpatory or impeaching)
2. The prosecution suppressed or withheld the evidence
3. The evidence is material (reasonable probability of different outcome)

### Post-Conviction Motions — Complete Procedure

**MCR 6.500 — Motion for Relief from Judgment:**
1. File in trial court; no time limit but one year is typical
2. Motion must: state grounds; cite authority; include verification
3. Grounds that can be raised: ineffective assistance of counsel; newly discovered evidence; constitutional violations; newly recognized right
4. **Procedural default rule (MCR 6.508(D)):** If ground was not raised on direct appeal, must show "good cause" for failure to raise AND actual prejudice (or fundamental miscarriage of justice)
5. Only one motion as of right; subsequent motions require leave of court showing new grounds
6. If trial court denies: appeal by leave to Court of Appeals; if COA denies: application to MSC

### Plea Agreements (MCR 6.302)

**Types of Plea Agreements:**

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Sentence agreement** | Prosecutor agrees to recommend (or not oppose) a specific sentence |
| **Charge bargain** | Prosecutor agrees to dismiss or reduce charges |
| **Cobbs evaluation** | Judge provides non-binding preliminary sentencing evaluation on defendant's request (*People v Cobbs*, 443 Mich 276 (1993)) |
| **Killebrew agreement** | Judge agrees to be bound by plea agreement sentencing terms (*People v Killebrew*, 416 Mich 189 (1982)) |

**Before accepting a guilty plea, court must confirm (MCR 6.302(B)):**
- Defendant understands nature of the charge
- Defendant understands the maximum possible prison term and any mandatory minimums
- Defendant is aware of and waives trial rights (jury trial, confrontation, self-incrimination)
- Plea is voluntary; no improper promises or threats
- Factual basis exists for the plea

**Withdrawal of Plea:**
- Before sentence: court may allow for any fair and just reason (MCR 6.310(B))
- After sentence: only to correct manifest injustice (MCR 6.310(C))

## 20. Standards of Review


Michigan appellate courts apply different standards of review depending on the nature of the issue. Correctly identifying and arguing the applicable standard is essential.

---

| Standard | Definition | Typical Application | Authority |
|---|---|---|---|
| **De novo** | Court reviews the issue anew, without deference to the lower court | Questions of law; constitutional questions; summary disposition; statutory interpretation; subject matter jurisdiction | *Maiden v Rozwood*, 461 Mich 109 (1999) |
| **Clearly erroneous** | Finding of fact is clearly erroneous if the reviewing court is left with a definite and firm conviction that a mistake was made | Findings of fact in bench trials; child custody/parenting time findings; TPR factual findings | MCR 2.613(C); *Vodvarka v Grasmeyer*, 259 Mich App 499 (2003) |
| **Abuse of discretion** | A decision is an abuse of discretion only if it falls outside the range of principled outcomes | Evidentiary rulings; sanctions; motions for new trial; sentence (non-guidelines) | *Barnett v Hidalgo*, 478 Mich 151 (2007) |
| **Preponderance of the evidence** | Standard of proof applied by trial court; not a standard of review per se | Burden of proof in most civil/family matters | — |
| **Substantial evidence** | Agency fact-finding sustained if supported by competent, material, and substantial evidence on the whole record | Administrative agency appeals | MCL 24.306 |
| **Plain error** | Unpreserved errors reviewed for plain error affecting substantial rights; reversal only to prevent manifest injustice | Unpreserved trial errors | *People v Carines*, 460 Mich 750 (1999); *Vushaj v Farm Bureau*, 284 Mich App 513 (2009) |
| **Beyond a reasonable doubt** | Highest standard; not a standard of review but of proof | Criminal conviction; criminal contempt; ICWA removal | US Const amend XIV; MCL 762.8 |
| **Clear and convincing evidence** | Highly probable; intermediate standard | TPR; civil commitment; fraud |  *Santosky v Kramer*, 455 US 745 (1982) |

**Mixed Questions of Law and Fact:**
- Legal conclusions drawn from facts: *de novo*
- Underlying facts: *clearly erroneous*
- Application of a legal standard to found facts: varies; often *de novo* for legal elements, *clearly erroneous* for factual underpinning

**Custody Specific:**
- Custody determination (which parent): *de novo* (questions of law embedded) and *clearly erroneous* (factual findings)
- Whether proper cause/change of circumstances found: *clearly erroneous* if factual; *de novo* if legal standard misapplied

> *Key case:* *Fletcher v Fletcher*, 447 Mich 871 (1994) (setting out abuse of discretion / clearly erroneous framework for custody).

---

### Preservation of Standard of Review

To preserve the applicable standard, a party must:
- **Object to legal errors** at the time of ruling → preserves de novo review
- **Object to factual findings** at or before entry of order → preserves clearly erroneous review
- **Challenge evidentiary rulings** at time evidence is offered → preserves abuse-of-discretion review
- **Challenge constitutional issues** in the trial court → preserves de novo review
- **Fail to object** → plain error review (4-prong *Carines* test)

**First-Instance vs. Second-Instance Review:**
When the Court of Appeals reviews a trial court decision de novo, the MSC reviews the Court of Appeals decision de novo as well. If the COA reviewed for clear error, the MSC still applies its own de novo standard to any embedded legal question but clear error to the COA's fact-review.

### Standards of Review — Criminal Context

| Issue | Standard | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Sufficiency of evidence | Whether rational trier of fact could find elements proven beyond a reasonable doubt | *People v Wolfe*, 440 Mich 508 (1992) |
| Sentencing departure (MSGCS post-*Lockridge*) | Reasonableness / abuse of discretion | *People v Lockridge*, 498 Mich 358 (2015) |
| Constitutional search/seizure questions | De novo (legal); clear error (factual) | *People v Kazmierczak*, 461 Mich 411 (2000) |
| Ineffective assistance of counsel | Mixed: de novo (legal standard); clearly erroneous (factual findings) | *Strickland v Washington*, 466 US 668 (1984); *People v Carbin*, 463 Mich 590 (2001) |
| Jury instruction error | De novo; if unpreserved, plain error | *People v Aldrich*, 246 Mich App 101 (2001) |
| Prosecutorial misconduct | De novo; if unpreserved, plain error | *People v Brown*, 279 Mich App 116 (2008) |
