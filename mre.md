# Michigan Rules of Evidence (MRE) — Complete Reference

**Abbreviation:** MRE  
**Promulgated by:** Michigan Supreme Court  
**Purpose:** Govern the admissibility of evidence in all Michigan courts  
**Citation format:** MRE [rule number] (e.g., MRE 803(6))  
**Note:** Michigan largely follows the Federal Rules of Evidence (FRE). Key differences are noted for each rule.

## Article I — General Provisions

| Rule | Title | Key Points | FRE Counterpart |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MRE 101** | Title and Scope | Rules govern all Michigan court proceedings unless otherwise specified. | FRE 101 |
| **MRE 102** | Purpose and Construction | Secure fairness; eliminate unjustifiable expense and delay; promote development of evidence law. | FRE 102 |
| **MRE 103** | Rulings on Evidence | Error not preserved without timely objection stating specific grounds; offer of proof required for exclusion error; plain error exception. | FRE 103 |
| **MRE 104** | Preliminary Questions | Court determines admissibility; not bound by evidence rules in doing so; conditional relevance resolved by jury. | FRE 104 |
| **MRE 105** | Limited Admissibility | When evidence admitted for limited purpose, court shall restrict scope and instruct jury upon request. | FRE 105 |

## Article II — Judicial Notice

| Rule | Title | Key Points | FRE Counterpart |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MRE 201** | Judicial Notice of Adjudicative Facts | Not subject to reasonable dispute: generally known in jurisdiction, or accurately and readily determinable. Court must take notice if requested; jury may accept or reject noticed fact in criminal cases. | FRE 201 |

| Rule | Title | Key Points | FRE Counterpart |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MRE 301** | Presumptions in Civil Actions | A presumption imposes burden of production (not burden of proof) on the party against whom it is directed. Bursting-bubble theory. | FRE 301 |
| **MRE 302** | Applicability of Federal Law | Federal law governs presumptions as to elements of federal claims in civil cases. | FRE 302 |

## Article IV — Relevancy and Its Limits

| Rule | Title | Key Points | FRE Counterpart |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MRE 401** | Definition of Relevant Evidence | Any tendency to make a fact of consequence more or less probable. Very low threshold. | FRE 401 |
| **MRE 402** | Relevant Evidence Generally Admissible | All relevant evidence is admissible unless excluded by rule, constitution, or statute. Irrelevant evidence inadmissible. | FRE 402 |
| **MRE 403** | Exclusion of Relevant Evidence | Excluded if probative value **substantially outweighed** by unfair prejudice, confusion, misleading jury, undue delay, waste of time, or cumulative evidence. | FRE 403 |
| **MRE 404** | Character Evidence; Other Crimes or Acts | (a) General ban on character propensity evidence; exceptions for defendant's and victim's character and witness credibility. (b) Other acts admissible for **MIMIC**: Motive, Opportunity, Intent, preparation, scheme/plan/system, Knowledge, Identity, absence of Mistake/accident. | FRE 404 |
| **MRE 405** | Methods of Proving Character | Reputation and opinion testimony when character admissible. Specific acts only when character is an essential element. | FRE 405 |
| **MRE 406** | Habit; Routine Practice | Habit or organizational routine is relevant to prove conduct on a specific occasion. | FRE 406 |
| **MRE 407** | Subsequent Remedial Measures | Not admissible to prove negligence, culpable conduct, or defect. Admissible for ownership, control, feasibility (if controverted), or impeachment. | FRE 407 |
| **MRE 408** | Compromise and Offers to Compromise | Settlement negotiations and offers inadmissible to prove liability or amount of claim. Admissible for other purposes (bias, obstruction). | FRE 408 |
| **MRE 409** | Payment of Medical Expenses | Offer to pay medical expenses inadmissible to prove liability. No "other purposes" exception in Michigan (unlike FRE 409). | FRE 409 |
| **MRE 410** | Inadmissibility of Pleas | Withdrawn guilty pleas, nolo contendere pleas, and plea discussion statements inadmissible against defendant. | FRE 410 |
| **MRE 411** | Liability Insurance | Insurance inadmissible to prove negligence or wrongfulness. Admissible for agency, ownership, control, bias. | FRE 411 |
| **MRE 412** | Rape Shield Law | Victim's past sexual conduct inadmissible in sexual assault cases. Exceptions: specific acts with defendant (consent defense); DNA/physical evidence of third-party; constitutional requirements. Special hearing required. | FRE 412 |

## Article V — Privileges

| Rule | Title | Key Points | FRE Counterpart |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MRE 501** | Privileges; General Rule | Privileges governed by common law principles as interpreted by Michigan courts, plus numerous statutory privileges (MCL 600.2157–2168). Key privileges: attorney-client, physician-patient, psychologist-patient, social worker, clergy-penitent, spousal, accountant, nurse-patient, school counselor. | FRE 501 |
| **MRE 502** | Attorney-Client Privilege; Work Product; Waiver | Voluntary disclosure waives privilege only as to undisclosed communications on the same subject matter if fairness requires. Inadvertent disclosure does not waive if reasonable precautions taken. | FRE 502 |

## Article VI — Witnesses

| Rule | Title | Key Points | FRE Counterpart |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MRE 601** | General Rule of Competency | Every person competent to testify unless otherwise provided. | FRE 601 |
| **MRE 602** | Lack of Personal Knowledge | Witness may only testify to matters within personal knowledge. Exception for experts (MRE 703). | FRE 602 |
| **MRE 603** | Oath or Affirmation | Every witness must declare they will testify truthfully; accommodates religious and non-religious witnesses. | FRE 603 |
| **MRE 604** | Interpreters | Interpreter must be qualified as expert and sworn to make true translation. | FRE 604 |
| **MRE 605** | Judge as Witness | Presiding judge may not testify at trial; no objection needed to preserve error. | FRE 605 |
| **MRE 606** | Juror as Witness | Juror may not testify at trial. Post-verdict testimony limited to extraneous prejudicial information or improper outside influence; not to deliberations. | FRE 606 |
| **MRE 607** | Who May Impeach | Any party, including the calling party, may impeach a witness. Voucher rule abolished. | FRE 607 |
| **MRE 608** | Character and Conduct of Witness | Credibility may be attacked by reputation or opinion for untruthfulness. Specific instances of untruthful conduct may be explored on cross-examination but no extrinsic proof. | FRE 608 |
| **MRE 609** | Impeachment by Prior Conviction | (1) Crime punishable by >1 year: admissible if probative value not substantially outweighed by prejudicial effect (to defendant). (2) Dishonesty/false statement (theft, fraud, perjury, etc.): admissible per se without balancing. 10-year limit; juvenile adjudications generally excluded. | FRE 609 |
| **MRE 610** | Religious Beliefs | Religious beliefs inadmissible to attack or support credibility. | FRE 610 |
| **MRE 611** | Mode and Order of Interrogation | Court controls mode and order of examination. Cross-examination limited to scope of direct and credibility. Leading questions on cross and hostile witnesses. | FRE 611 |
| **MRE 612** | Writing Used to Refresh Memory | If writing used to refresh memory, adverse party entitled to see it and cross-examine on it. | FRE 612 |
| **MRE 613** | Prior Statements of Witnesses | Extrinsic proof of prior inconsistent statement admissible only if witness afforded opportunity to explain or deny. Prior consistent statements admissible only to rebut charge of recent fabrication. | FRE 613 |
| **MRE 614** | Court-Called Witnesses | Court may call witnesses on its own motion; all parties may cross-examine. Court may interrogate any witness. | FRE 614 |
| **MRE 615** | Exclusion of Witnesses | Court shall exclude witnesses upon request. Exceptions: parties (natural persons), party's representative, essential witness, crime victim. | FRE 615 |

## Article VII — Opinions and Expert Testimony

| Rule | Title | Key Points | FRE Counterpart |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MRE 701** | Lay Witness Opinion | Limited to: rationally based on perception; helpful to understanding testimony or determining fact; not based on scientific/technical/specialized knowledge. | FRE 701 |
| **MRE 702** | Expert Witness Testimony | Expert may testify if: (1) based on sufficient facts or data; (2) product of reliable principles and methods (**Daubert** standard, adopted 2004); (3) principles and methods reliably applied to the facts. Replaced the older *Frye* general-acceptance test. | FRE 702 |
| **MRE 703** | Bases of Expert Opinion | Expert may rely on facts/data not admitted in evidence if experts in the field would reasonably rely on such data. | FRE 703 |
| **MRE 704** | Opinion on Ultimate Issue | Opinion testimony not objectionable merely because it embraces an ultimate issue. No specific prohibition on mental state opinions in criminal cases (unlike FRE 704(b)). | FRE 704 |
| **MRE 705** | Disclosing Underlying Facts or Data | Expert may state opinion without first testifying to the basis; basis may be explored on cross-examination. | FRE 705 |
| **MRE 706** | Court-Appointed Experts | Court may appoint neutral experts agreed upon by parties or chosen by court; subject to deposition and cross-examination. | FRE 706 |

## Article VIII — Hearsay

### Definitions (MRE 801)

| Concept | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Statement** | An oral or written assertion, or nonverbal conduct intended as an assertion. |
| **Declarant** | A person who makes a statement. |
| **Hearsay** | An out-of-court statement offered to prove the truth of the matter asserted. |
| **Non-Hearsay: Prior Inconsistent Statement** | A prior inconsistent statement made under oath at a prior proceeding is not hearsay. |
| **Non-Hearsay: Prior Consistent Statement** | A prior consistent statement offered to rebut a charge of recent fabrication or improper motive is not hearsay. |
| **Non-Hearsay: Party-Opponent Admission** | A statement made by a party, an authorized spokesperson, a co-conspirator, or an agent (within scope of employment) is not hearsay when offered against that party. |

### General Rule (MRE 802)

**Hearsay is not admissible** unless an exception applies under the MRE or another Supreme Court rule.

### MRE 803 — Hearsay Exceptions (Declarant's Availability Immaterial)

| Exception | Rule | Summary |
|---|---|---|
| Present Sense Impression | **MRE 803(1)** | Statement describing or explaining an event **while** or **immediately after** the declarant perceived it. |
| Excited Utterance | **MRE 803(2)** | Statement relating to a startling event, made while the declarant was **under the stress of excitement** it caused. |
| Then-Existing Mental/Emotional/Physical State | **MRE 803(3)** | Declarant's then-existing state of mind (motive, intent, plan) or physical condition (pain, health). **Not** to prove the fact remembered. |
| Medical Diagnosis or Treatment | **MRE 803(4)** | Statements to physician describing medical history, symptoms, pain, or cause of injury, made for purposes of diagnosis or treatment. |
| Recorded Recollection | **MRE 803(5)** | Record of a matter the witness once knew but now cannot recall, made when matter was fresh in memory and reflecting that knowledge accurately. Read into evidence; not received as exhibit unless offered by adverse party. |
| Business Records | **MRE 803(6)** | Record of an act, event, condition, opinion, or diagnosis: made at or near the time; by someone with knowledge; kept in the ordinary course of a regularly conducted activity; making was a regular practice; foundational testimony provided. Untrustworthy records excluded. |
| Absence of Business Record | **MRE 803(7)** | Evidence that a matter is not in a regularly kept record, if introduction of the record would otherwise be admissible. |
| Public Records | **MRE 803(8)** | Records of public offices setting out the office's activities, matters observed while under a legal duty to report (not law enforcement observations in criminal cases), or factual findings from a legal investigation. |
| Public Records of Vital Statistics | **MRE 803(9)** | Records of birth, fetal death, death, or marriage reported to a public office. |
| Absence of Public Record | **MRE 803(10)** | Testimony or certification that a diligent search failed to disclose a public record. |
| Religious Organization Records | **MRE 803(11)** | Statements of birth, marriage, death, etc. in regularly kept records of religious organizations. |
| Marriage/Baptism Certificates | **MRE 803(12)** | Statement of fact in a certificate by an authorized person who performed the ceremony. |
| Family Records | **MRE 803(13)** | Statements of personal or family history in family Bibles, genealogies, engravings, and similar records. |
| Records Affecting Property Interest | **MRE 803(14)** | Record of a document purporting to establish or affect a property interest. |
| Statements in Documents Affecting Property | **MRE 803(15)** | Statement in a document affecting property interest if relevant to the document's purpose. |
| Ancient Documents | **MRE 803(16)** | Statements in documents prepared before January 1, 1998, whose authenticity is established. |
| Market Reports; Commercial Publications | **MRE 803(17)** | Market quotations, directories, and other compilations generally relied on by the public. |
| Learned Treatises | **MRE 803(18)** | Statements in reliable treatises or periodicals, called to an expert's attention on cross or relied on in direct. Read into evidence; not received as an exhibit. |
| Reputation re: Personal/Family History | **MRE 803(19)** | Reputation among family or community concerning personal or family history. |
| Reputation re: Boundaries/General History | **MRE 803(20)** | Reputation in a community concerning land boundaries or general historical events. |
| Reputation re: Character | **MRE 803(21)** | Reputation among associates or in the community concerning a person's character. |
| Judgment of Previous Conviction | **MRE 803(22)** | Final judgment of conviction for a crime punishable by >1 year, offered to prove an essential fact in the judgment, against a party to the action. |
| Judgment re: Personal/Family History or Boundaries | **MRE 803(23)** | Judgment proving matters of personal or family history or boundaries that were essential to the judgment and could be proved by reputation evidence. |

### MRE 804 — Hearsay Exceptions (Declarant Unavailable)

**Unavailability** includes: exempt from testifying (privilege); refuses to testify despite court order; testifies to lack of memory; dead, ill, or infirm; absent despite good-faith effort to procure.

| Exception | Rule | Summary |
|---|---|---|
| Former Testimony | **MRE 804(b)(1)** | Testimony at a prior trial, hearing, or deposition, if the adverse party had an opportunity and similar motive to develop the testimony. |
| Dying Declaration | **MRE 804(b)(2)** | Statement made while believing death was imminent, about its cause or circumstances. Admissible in civil cases and homicide prosecutions. |
| Statement Against Interest | **MRE 804(b)(3)** | Statement so contrary to the declarant's proprietary, pecuniary, or penal interest that a reasonable person would not have made it unless they believed it to be true. |
| Statement of Personal/Family History | **MRE 804(b)(4)** | Statement about the declarant's own birth, adoption, marriage, or similar facts, even without personal knowledge of the fact. |
| Forfeiture by Wrongdoing | **MRE 804(b)(5)** | Statement offered against a party that intentionally caused or acquiesced in causing the declarant's unavailability. |

---

### MRE 805–807

| Rule | Title | Summary |
|---|---|---|
| **MRE 805** | Hearsay Within Hearsay | Double (or layered) hearsay is admissible only if each layer satisfies a hearsay exception. |
| **MRE 806** | Attacking Declarant's Credibility | When hearsay is admitted, the declarant's credibility may be attacked or supported as if the declarant had testified. |
| **MRE 807** | Residual Exception | A hearsay statement not covered by MRE 803 or 804 may be admitted if: (1) equivalent trustworthiness; (2) evidence of a material fact; (3) more probative than other available evidence; (4) best serves justice. **Pre-trial notice required.** |

## Article IX — Authentication and Identification

| Rule | Title | Key Points | FRE Counterpart |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MRE 901** | Requirement of Authentication | Proponent must produce evidence sufficient to support a finding that the item is what the proponent claims. Examples: witness testimony; non-distinctive characteristics + circumstantial evidence; chain of custody; expert comparison; voice identification; phone conversations; public records; ancient documents; process/system (electronic records). | FRE 901 |
| **MRE 902** | Self-Authentication | No extrinsic evidence needed for: domestic public documents under seal; domestic public documents not under seal; foreign public documents; certified copies of public records; official publications; newspapers/periodicals; trade inscriptions/labels; acknowledged instruments; commercial paper; certified business records. | FRE 902 |
| **MRE 903** | Subscribing Witness' Testimony | Subscribing witness testimony required only when the law of the jurisdiction whose law governs the document's validity requires it. | FRE 903 |

## Article X — Contents of Writings, Recordings, and Photographs (Best Evidence Rule)

| Rule | Title | Key Points | FRE Counterpart |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MRE 1001** | Definitions | Writing; recording; photograph (includes X-rays, video, film); original; duplicate. | FRE 1001 |
| **MRE 1002** | Requirement of the Original | An original writing, recording, or photograph is required to prove its **content**. Rule does not apply to facts independent of the writing. | FRE 1002 |
| **MRE 1003** | Admissibility of Duplicates | A duplicate is admissible unless: genuine question raised about original's authenticity; or it would be unfair to admit the duplicate. | FRE 1003 |
| **MRE 1004** | Admissibility of Other Evidence of Content | Secondary evidence admissible if: originals lost or destroyed (not in bad faith); original cannot be obtained by judicial process; opponent in control and on notice fails to produce; or writing is collateral to a controlling issue. | FRE 1004 |
| **MRE 1005** | Copies of Public Records | Certified copy or testimony of a witness who compared the copy to the original is admissible to prove content of an official record. | FRE 1005 |
| **MRE 1006** | Summaries | Summary, chart, or calculation may be used to prove voluminous writings; originals must be made available to opposing parties. | FRE 1006 |
| **MRE 1007** | Testimony of Party | Content of a writing may be proved by the opponent party's testimony, deposition, or written statement. | FRE 1007 |
| **MRE 1008** | Functions of Court and Jury | Court decides preliminary questions about authenticity, loss/destruction, and notice. Jury determines whether the writing existed, and whether other evidence reflects its content. | FRE 1008 |

## Quick Reference: Key Differences Between MRE and FRE

| Topic | MRE | FRE |
|---|---|---|
| Judicial Notice of Law | MRE 202 (explicit rule) | No FRE 202; federal courts take notice of law implicitly |
| Expert Testimony Standard | Daubert (adopted 2004 after *Gilbert v. DaimlerChrysler*) | Daubert (FRE 702) |
| Rape Shield | MRE 412 (somewhat broader) | FRE 412 |
| Mental State Opinion by Expert in Criminal Case | No restriction (unlike FRE 704(b)) | FRE 704(b) prohibits expert mental state opinions on ultimate issue in criminal cases |
| Privileges | Governed by Michigan common law and numerous MCL statutes | FRE 501: federal common law controls in federal proceedings |
| Ancient Documents Cutoff | January 1, 1998 | January 1, 1998 (same, amended in 2017) |
